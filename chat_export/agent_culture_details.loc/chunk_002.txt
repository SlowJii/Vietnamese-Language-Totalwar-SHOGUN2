[147] Ishin shishi are “men of higher purpose” devoted to one thing: the reinstatement of Imperial divine rule. Champions of the Emperor, they rally and inspire the loyal whilst stirring unrest amongst rivals. Single-minded and dangerous, they will not hesitate to kill any who stand in their way.

[150] Shinobi are masters of subterfuge; they conduct espionage, sabotage and assassinations. Their ability to infiltrate and observe also makes them invaluable army scouts and enables the establishment of spy networks in their clan’s settlements.
[151] Shirabyoshi are traditional dancers who perform for nobles on formal occasions. Dressed as men in formal Shinto clothes and white powdered make-up, the slow, rhythmic dances they perform in veneration of the gods also involve poetry, singing and musicianship. The shirabyoshi’s main skill is seduction, which convinces her target to join her lord’s cause. Shirabyoshi are able to entertain the nobles of friendly towns in order to boost their growth and entertain friendly generals in the field, which increases their loyalty to the family and counteracts the efforts of enemy agents to bribe or seduce them into changing sides.
[152] Foreign veterans are masters of warfare having fought, and survived, in many battles. Their long years of service make them great military instructors and administrators; their expertise makes them keen marksmen and sappers, as well as fearless duellists.
[154] Shinsengumi are enforcers of the Shogun’s justice, coercing or subduing those who dare oppose his will. They will resort to any means necessary to prevent Imperialists from overthrowing the Shogunate, even assassinating their enemies and stirring dissidents to open revolt.
[155] Junsatsushi ensure loyalty and obedience from friendly generals, increase income by preventing corruption, keep our castle towns and armies safe from enemy subterfuge when positioned inside them, and can request the allegiance of neutral and enemy castle towns, along with any forces garrisoned there. Junsatsushi can also pay off rival agents.
[159] Monomi act as assassins, spies, saboteurs and scouts, and are recruited from province speciality buildings. In addition to their subterfuge skills, monomi also increase the movement and line of sight of our armies when placed inside them. They can also establish spy networks within castle towns, which increase the chances of discovering any rival agents and armies that may be hiding within a province. Monomi are invisible to the enemy until detected.
