[400] Veteran units are added to the standard troop roster as your campaign progresses. If a unit excels in battle, you will be able to recruit it as a veteran. It is then available on the veteran units tab for the rest of your campaign. It will gain experience as it fights. The number of veterans you can recruit is limited, but increases as your avatar levels up,  as shown by the highlighted slots on the veteran units tab.
[401] Completing an armour set will unlock a prized retainer for your army.
[402] Entering into diplomacy gives the clan a means to negotiate matters of alliance, war and trade. To begin negotiations with another clan, select one from the drop-down list attached to the radar map and double-click on it. Alternatively, click on the diplomacy button in the bottom right-hand side of the screen and then select a clan from there.
[403] As they gain experience veteran units gain new skills and abilities through the skills button on the unit information panel. Veteran casualties persist from battle to battle and can be monitored on the panel. A veteran unit regains full strength if it is left out of a battle.
[404] As it is possible to call in fire support from a nearby fleet you can issue a bombardment order with the naval fire support button on the battle review panel. After clicking on it, simply left-click on the area of the battlefield you wish to bombard. The command to launch fire support can be cancelled by right-clicking anywhere on the land or sea away from the user interface. Fire support is a naturally inaccurate business, so friendly units stood nearby may suffer collateral damage.
[405] The wealth of a province is the sum of the base income generated by the settlement, farms, ports and province speciality buildings, minus tax. This net income is displayed in the lower left section of the province details panel. The lower right section shows the factors affecting the settlement's additional economic growth over the following turn and indeed every turn to come.
[406] Many provinces have special buildings: schools, mines, holy sites. These resources can be developed, traded, or are used to construct advanced buildings. All are worth having.

[422] Ishin shishi can persuade a general or the garrison of a settlement to switch allegiance to our cause. If successful, there is a chance that one or more of his units will follow him, also changing sides. Persuading a neutral army anywhere is tantamount to a declaration of war against their clan. Following successful persuasion, an army on neutral or friendly territory will retreat to our territory unless we have a diplomatic agreement for military access in place. Simply select our ishin shishi and right-click on a target army to attempt persuasion.
[423] Select a technology you wish to acquire. [425] Each general has a loyalty rating that is used to determine his likeliness to be bribed, leave our clan and defect to another. If their loyalty gets too low there may be no choice other than to order that they commit seppuku.
