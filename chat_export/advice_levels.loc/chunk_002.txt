[102] If you attack a port whilst a fleet is moored there you will have to fight a port battle. This is effectively a siege, where the docked fleet is the defender, bolstered by coastal defences.
[103] The geisha is a traditional entertainer who uses her skills to enchant, inspire, entertain and distract. A geisha can use her enchantment skills to convince a target to join our cause. They are able to entertain the nobles of friendly towns in order to boost their growth and inspire friendly generals in the field, which increases their loyalty and counteracts the efforts of enemy agents to bribe or enchant them into changing sides. See the characters section of the encyclopaedia for further information.
[104] The Boshin War is a conflict between the forces of the Shogunate and those of the Emperor. Every clan must pledge allegiance to one or the other. The object of the campaign game is to secure allegiance to our cause and capture territory. When our clan’s fame reaches a certain level we must choose to rally behind one power or the other, or attempt to assume personal control of the country. This last option will lead to all but our most loyal allies turning against us.
[105] As different skills have different costs, when spending a skill point you will increase the cost of fielding this unit. All cost changes can be seen on the skill’s tooltip. When ready, press the tick button to return to the veteran tab.
[106] A settlement is the main administrative centre of a province. Classed as town, large town, city, municipality and prefecture depending on size, it also contains trade and military buildings.
[107] Funds are deducted from our treasury at the beginning of each turn to pay for the supplies and wages of the clan's troops. This is known as unit upkeep.
[108] The summary tab is used to display important information about the state of our clan, such as bonus effects and clan fame, in one place for ease of reference. Mouse over them to reveal further details.

Select one to move there instantly. It is also possible to use stations belonging to those clans with whom military access has been negotiated.
[111] When attacking an enemy that is deployed on a hill, attack up the most gentle slope, even if it takes a while to get there. Fewer troops will be exhausted by the time they reach the top of the slope.

This is simulated by the fog of war. Use our forces to reveal the machinations of rivals before they act against us!
